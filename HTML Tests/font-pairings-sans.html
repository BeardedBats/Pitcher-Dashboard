Font Pairing Options — Sans-Serif

DM Sans (headers) paired with five sans-serif body fonts. No monospace — all proportional, all clean.

Option 6 DM Sans + Nunito Sans

Rounded terminals and generous x-height give Nunito Sans a warm, approachable feel — softening the data-heavy table without sacrificing legibility. The roundness contrasts nicely with DM Sans' sharper geometry. Great if you want the dashboard to feel less intimidating and more inviting. font-family: 'Nunito Sans', sans-serif

Option 7 DM Sans + Work Sans

Designed specifically for on-screen text at small to medium sizes. Work Sans has a slightly condensed width that lets long pitcher names breathe while keeping columns tight. The subtle quirkiness in letterforms (the lowercase 'a' and 'g') adds character without being distracting. font-family: 'Work Sans', sans-serif

Option 8 DM Sans + Plus Jakarta Sans

A geometric sans with soft, slightly rounded strokes — sharing DNA with DM Sans but with a tighter, more compact rhythm. This pairing creates visual harmony since both fonts come from the same geometric tradition, while Jakarta's narrower proportions keep the table data visually distinct from the headers. font-family: 'Plus Jakarta Sans', sans-serif

Option 9 DM Sans + Outfit

A variable geometric sans with clean, open letterforms and excellent number rendering. Outfit's wide apertures and even stroke widths make digits extremely clear at small sizes — ideal for dense stat tables. The geometric construction complements DM Sans while the slightly taller x-height creates just enough visual separation. font-family: 'Outfit', sans-serif

Option 10 DM Sans + Manrope

A semi-rounded, modern grotesque with optical size adjustments baked in — digits and letters are optimized for small UI contexts. Manrope has a subtle warmth (rounded stroke endings) without the full softness of Nunito Sans, landing in a sweet spot between clinical and friendly. Tabular figures are built-in. font-family: 'Manrope', sans-serif
